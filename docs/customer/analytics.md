# Analytics Dashboard

Track the performance of your social media content across every connected platform from a single dashboard. The **Analytics** page gives you a complete picture of views, engagement, follower growth, and optimal posting times -- so you can spend more time creating content and less time digging through individual platform dashboards.

<!-- screenshot: The Analytics page showing the Overview tab with KPI sparkline cards (Views, Followers, Clicks, Engagement Rate) and the area trend chart below -->

---

## Getting there

From any page, click **Analytics** in the left sidebar. The dashboard opens to the **Overview** tab with the last 30 days of data selected by default.

---

## Syncing your data

Social Suite pulls performance data from every connected platform automatically. A **Sync Status** badge in the top-right corner shows when the last sync happened.

If you need the very latest numbers right now, click the **Sync Now** button. A spinning indicator appears while the sync runs; when it finishes, every chart and table on the page refreshes automatically.

## Choosing a date range

Use the date-range bar at the top to control which time period the dashboard covers.

### Quick presets

Click any of the preset buttons to jump to a common window:

| Button | Time window |
|--------|-------------|
| **7D** | Last 7 days |
| **14D** | Last 14 days |
| **30D** | Last 30 days (default) |
| **90D** | Last 90 days |
| **Since Signup** | From the day your company was created |
| **All Time** | Up to 2 years of historical data |

### Custom range

Click the **calendar button** (to the right of the presets) to open a two-month calendar picker. Select a start date and an end date, and the dashboard updates instantly.

## Dashboard tabs

The analytics page is organized into six tabs. Click a tab name to switch views.

### Overview

Your high-level performance snapshot.

- **KPI cards** -- four cards across the top show **Views**, **Followers**, **Clicks**, and **Engagement Rate**. Each card includes a sparkline (a mini trend line) and a percentage change compared to the previous period.
- **Views & Engagement Trend** -- an area chart that plots daily views and engagement over the selected date range.
- **Conversion Funnel** -- a funnel visualization showing how impressions narrow down to views, clicks, and engagements.

<!-- screenshot: The Overview tab showing four KPI sparkline cards and the area trend chart -->

### Platforms

See how each connected platform contributes to your overall numbers.

- **Follower Distribution** -- a donut chart showing the share of followers per platform (e.g., Instagram 45%, Facebook 30%, LinkedIn 25%).
- **Engagement by Platform** -- a treemap that sizes each platform block by its total engagement.
- **Engagement Rate by Platform** -- gauge charts comparing each platform's engagement rate.
- **Platform Metrics Overview** -- a detailed metrics matrix breaking down each platform's key numbers side by side.

<!-- screenshot: The Platforms tab showing the donut chart, treemap, and gauge widget -->

### Engagement

Understand how your audience interacts with your content and when they are most active.

- **Content Decay** -- a bar chart that shows how quickly engagement drops off after a post is published (grouped into time buckets like 0-6 hours, 6-12 hours, etc.).
- **Best Times to Post** -- a heatmap showing engagement levels by day of the week and hour of the day. Brighter cells mean higher engagement.
- **Optimal Posting Times** -- a card that ranks the top posting windows per platform, based on your historical data. Each recommendation shows the day, hour, average engagement rate, and a confidence level (High Confidence, Confident, or Low Confidence) depending on how much data is available.

<!-- screenshot: The Engagement tab with the heatmap and the Optimal Posting Times card -->

### Audience

Track how your follower base is growing over time.

- **Total Followers** -- a KPI card with a sparkline showing your current total and recent trend.
- **Net Change** -- the number of followers gained (or lost) over the selected period.
- **Follower Growth** -- an area chart plotting daily follower counts.

<!-- screenshot: The Audience tab with the follower growth area chart -->

### All Posts

A searchable, sortable table of every published post and its performance metrics.

For each post, the table shows:

| Column | What it means |
|--------|---------------|
| **Post** | A preview of the post text and publish date |
| **Platforms** | Platform icons for each network the post was published to |
| **Views** | Total views/impressions |
| **Engagement** | Likes, comments, and shares (with icons) |
| **Rate** | Engagement rate as a percentage |
| **Status** | Published, Draft, Unpublished, etc. |
| **Source** | Whether the post was created through Longtale or directly on the platform |

**Sort presets** -- use the buttons above the table to quickly sort by **Most Recent**, **Most Views**, **Most Engagement**, **Highest Rate**, **Most Comments**, or **Most Shares**. Click the same button again to reverse the sort order.

**Search** -- type in the search bar to filter posts by content keywords.

**Export** -- click the **Export** button in the toolbar to download the table as a CSV file.

If a post was published to multiple platforms, click the expand arrow to see per-platform breakdowns.

<!-- screenshot: The All Posts table with sort preset buttons and a post row expanded to show per-platform stats -->

### Outlets

If your organization is a media company with multiple child outlets, this tab shows a side-by-side comparison of each outlet's performance: followers, engagement, post count, and reach. A relative engagement bar lets you quickly spot which outlets are leading.

This tab only appears for media company accounts with child outlets configured.

<!-- screenshot: The Outlets tab showing cards for three child outlets with metric breakdowns -->

## Tips

- **Check the Engagement tab before scheduling posts.** The heatmap and optimal posting windows are based on your own data, so the recommendations get more accurate the more you publish.
- **Use date presets to compare periods.** Switch between 7D and 30D to spot short-term spikes vs. longer trends.
- **Sort the All Posts table by Engagement Rate** to find content that resonated most with your audience, regardless of reach.
- **Export regularly.** The CSV export is useful for sharing performance reports with clients or stakeholders who do not have access to Social Suite.
- **Sync before a meeting.** If you need up-to-the-minute numbers for a presentation, hit **Sync Now** a few minutes beforehand.

## FAQ

**How often is analytics data updated?**
Data syncs automatically every hour from your connected platforms. You can also trigger an on-demand sync by clicking **Sync Now**.

**Why do some posts show zero views?**
Some platforms do not report view counts for certain post types (e.g., text-only tweets). In those cases, you will see zero in the Views column, but engagement metrics (likes, comments, shares) will still appear.

**Can I filter analytics by a single platform?**
Yes. On the **Platforms** tab, the Platform Metrics Overview breaks down each platform individually. On the **All Posts** tab, you can use the search bar to find posts from a specific platform, or sort/filter the grid columns.

**What does "Since Signup" mean?**
It sets the date range to start from the day your company was created in Social Suite, so you see your entire history.

**What is the Conversion Funnel?**
It shows how your content metrics flow from broad (impressions) to narrow (clicks and engagements). It helps you understand where in the funnel you are losing audience attention.

**Can I export charts as images?**
Currently, only the All Posts table supports CSV export. To capture charts, use your operating system's screenshot tool.
