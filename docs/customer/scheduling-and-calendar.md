# Scheduling and Calendar

Plan your content ahead of time using Social Suite's scheduling and calendar features. See your upcoming posts at a glance, manage drafts, and keep your publishing cadence consistent.

---

## Scheduling a Post

You can schedule a post during the compose workflow:

1. Complete all steps of the compose wizard (see [Creating Content](creating-content.md)).
2. In the final **Compose & Preview** step, instead of clicking **Publish Now**, use the scheduling controls:
   - Click the **calendar icon** to pick a date.
   - Enter a time in the **time picker** (24-hour format, e.g., 14:30).
3. Click **Schedule**.

The post is saved and will be published automatically at the date and time you selected.

## The Calendar View

To see all your scheduled posts on a timeline:

1. Go to **Content** in the left sidebar.
2. Click the **Calendar** tab.

### Weekly Grid

The calendar displays the current week (Monday through Sunday) with time slots from 6:00 AM to 11:00 AM. Each scheduled post appears as a colored block in the matching day and hour slot:

- The block color matches the platform (e.g., blue for Twitter, pink for Instagram, dark blue for LinkedIn).
- The block shows the platform icon and the scheduled time.

The current day is highlighted so you can quickly spot today's posts.

A label at the top of the grid shows the date range for the displayed week (e.g., "Mar 2 - 8, 2026").

<!-- screenshot: The weekly calendar grid showing colored post blocks on different days with platform icons -->

### Upcoming Posts Sidebar

To the right of the calendar grid, the **Upcoming Posts** panel lists all scheduled posts in chronological order. Each entry shows:

- The platform icon and color
- A preview of the post text
- The scheduled date and time (e.g., "Mar 5 at 2:30 PM")

If there are no scheduled posts, the panel shows a prompt to use the **Compose** tab to schedule your first post.

## Managing Drafts

Drafts are posts that you started composing but have not yet published or scheduled.

### Viewing Drafts

1. Go to **Content** > **Drafts** tab.
2. Each draft card shows:
   - **Source type** -- an article icon for article-based posts, a pen icon for posts from scratch.
   - **Title** -- the article title or "Post from Scratch."
   - **Current step** -- where you left off in the wizard (e.g., "Source", "Objective", "Channels", "Compose").
   - **Objective** -- the goal you selected (Reach, Engagement, or Clicks).
   - **Platform count** -- how many platforms have generated content.
   - **Last updated** -- a relative timestamp (e.g., "Updated 3 hours ago").

<!-- screenshot: The Drafts tab showing multiple draft cards with status badges and Continue buttons -->

### Resuming a Draft

Click **Continue** on any draft card. The compose wizard reopens at the step where you left off, with all your previous selections restored.

### Deleting a Draft

1. Click the **trash icon** on the draft card.
2. Confirm in the dialog that appears.
3. The draft is permanently deleted.

## Post Status Indicators

Posts move through the following statuses:

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Draft** | Still being composed; not yet submitted or scheduled |
| **Scheduled** | Set to publish at a future date and time |
| **Awaiting Approval** | Submitted for review; waiting on a reviewer |
| **Approved** | Reviewer approved the post; ready to publish |
| **Rejected** | Reviewer rejected the post |
| **Published** | Live on the platform |

## Tips

- **Schedule during high-engagement windows.** Check your analytics to find when your audience is most active, then schedule posts for those times.
- **Use the calendar to spot gaps.** If you see empty days on the weekly grid, consider scheduling content to maintain a consistent presence.
- **Clean up old drafts.** The Drafts tab can accumulate unfinished posts over time. Delete any you no longer plan to use.
- **Auto-save is your safety net.** If your browser closes unexpectedly, your in-progress post is saved as a draft. Check the Drafts tab to recover it.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I reschedule a post after it has been scheduled?**
Currently, rescheduling is not available through the calendar view. To change the time, you would need to create a new post with the updated schedule.

**Q: What time zone does scheduling use?**
Scheduling uses your browser's local time zone. The date and time you select are interpreted as your local time.

**Q: How far in advance can I schedule a post?**
There is no limit. You can schedule posts days, weeks, or months into the future.

**Q: Will I be notified when a scheduled post is published?**
Yes. You receive an in-app notification when a scheduled post goes live.

**Q: What happens if my account gets disconnected before a scheduled post publishes?**
The post will fail to publish. Reconnect the platform account under **Connections** and create a new scheduled post.
