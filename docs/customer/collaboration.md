# Team Collaboration

Social Suite is built for teams. Whether you are a solo social media manager getting approval from an editor or part of a large publishing team with multiple reviewers, the collaboration features keep everyone aligned without endless email threads.

---

## Content Approval Workflow

Every post in Social Suite moves through a clear lifecycle: **Draft > Awaiting Approval > Approved > Scheduled > Published**. This means nothing goes live without the right person signing off.

### How the Workflow Works

A post can be in any of these statuses:

| Status | What it means |
|--------|--------------|
| **Draft** | Being written or edited. Only the author can see it in their drafts. |
| **Awaiting Approval** | Submitted for review. The assigned reviewer receives a notification. |
| **Approved** | A reviewer has approved it. Ready to be scheduled. |
| **Rejected** | A reviewer has sent it back with feedback. The author can revise and resubmit. |
| **Scheduled** | Queued for publishing at a specific date and time. |
| **Published** | Live on the connected social platform. |
| **Pulled** | Removed from circulation after publishing. |

<!-- screenshot: A draft post card showing the status badge (e.g., "Awaiting Approval") with the reviewer name and submission timestamp -->

### Submitting a Post for Approval

1. Open the post you want reviewed from the **Content > Drafts** tab.
2. Click **Submit for Approval**.
3. Optionally, choose a specific **Reviewer** from the team member dropdown. If you do not choose one, any team member with publishing permissions can review it.
4. The post status changes to **Awaiting Approval** and the reviewer receives an in-app notification.

<!-- screenshot: The Submit for Approval dialog showing the optional reviewer picker and the Submit button -->

### Reviewing and Approving a Post

If you have publishing permissions (Owner or Admin role), you can approve or reject posts submitted by your team:

1. Look for posts with the **Awaiting Approval** status in your drafts list or notifications.
2. Open the post to review its content across all selected platforms.
3. Click **Approve** to move the post to the **Approved** status. The author receives a notification that their content was approved.
4. Or click **Reject** and add a reason. The author will see your feedback and can revise the post.

A rejected post goes back to **Draft** status so the author can make changes and resubmit.

<!-- screenshot: An approval review screen showing the post content, Approve and Reject buttons, and the rejection reason text field -->

### Approving by Email (External Approval)

You do not need to be logged in to approve a post. When an automation generates posts from your RSS feed, Social Suite sends an approval email with a unique link.

1. Open the email titled "Post Approval Request."
2. Review the generated content for each platform (the email shows previews for LinkedIn, Instagram, etc.).
3. Click **Review & Approve** to open the approval page in your browser.
4. On the approval page, click **Approve** to publish or close the page to leave it pending.

The approval link expires after 7 days. If it expires, log in to Social Suite to approve the post manually.

## Assigning Posts to Team Members

You can assign any draft to a specific team member so they know it is their responsibility.

1. Open a draft post.
2. Click **Assign** and select a team member from the dropdown.
3. The assigned person receives a notification saying "A draft has been assigned to you."

Assignments are visible on the draft card so everyone can see who is responsible for what.

## @Mentions in Conversations

When collaborating on inbox conversations, you can mention teammates in internal notes to bring something to their attention.

### How to @Mention a Teammate

1. Open a conversation in the **Inbox**.
2. Switch to **Note** mode (click the sticky note icon in the message composer). Internal notes are only visible to your team -- the contact never sees them.
3. Type **@** followed by the person's name. An autocomplete dropdown appears with matching team members.
4. Select the person from the dropdown. Their name is inserted into the note.
5. Press **Send**. The mentioned person receives an in-app notification.

<!-- screenshot: The message composer in note mode showing the @mention autocomplete dropdown with team member names -->

**Important:** @mentions only work in **note mode**, not in public replies. This keeps internal discussions private.

## Emoji Reactions on Messages

React to inbox messages with emojis to acknowledge them without typing a full reply. This is useful for quick feedback like "got it" or "nice job."

### How to React

1. Open a conversation in the **Inbox**.
2. Hover over any message to reveal the reaction options.
3. Click one of the available reactions: thumbs up, heart, laughing face, surprised face, sad face, or celebration.
4. Your reaction appears below the message. Other team members can see who reacted and add their own.
5. Click your reaction again to remove it.

Reactions show a count and the names of people who reacted when you hover over them.

## Read Receipts

See who on your team has read an inbox conversation. Read receipts appear as small avatar icons, so you know at a glance whether a teammate has seen a message.

- Avatars of team members who have read the conversation appear next to it in the conversation list.
- Hover over the avatars to see each person's name and when they last viewed it.
- Your own read status is not shown to you (only other team members').

## Notification Preferences

You can control which collaboration events trigger notifications and how you receive them.

Go to **Settings > Notifications** to configure:

| Event | Description |
|-------|------------|
| **Assignment** | When a draft or conversation is assigned to you |
| **@Mention** | When someone mentions you in an internal note |
| **Reply** | When someone replies to a conversation you are following |
| **Status change** | When a post's approval status changes |
| **Correction** | When a correction is requested on your content |
| **Escalation** | When a conversation is escalated to your attention |
| **SLA breach** | When a response time target is missed |

For each event, you can toggle **In-App** notifications (the bell icon in the sidebar) and **Email** notifications independently.

## Tips

- **Use notes for internal discussion.** Switch to note mode before typing anything you do not want the contact to see. Notes are highlighted differently so you can tell them apart from public replies.

- **Assign posts early.** Assigning a draft as soon as it is created keeps your team organized and avoids duplicate work.

- **Let email approvals do the heavy lifting.** If you manage automations that generate posts from RSS feeds, you can approve them right from your phone via the email link -- no need to open the app.

- **Check your notification settings once.** The defaults are sensible, but if you find yourself getting too many (or too few) alerts, adjust them in Settings. You can turn off email notifications for low-priority events and keep in-app only.

- **React instead of replying.** A quick thumbs-up reaction is faster than typing "sounds good" and keeps the conversation thread clean.

## FAQ

**Q: Who can approve posts?**
A: Only team members with the Owner or Admin role have publishing permissions. Members can create and submit posts but cannot approve or reject them.

**Q: Can I approve a post without logging in?**
A: Yes. Automation-generated posts send an approval email with a unique link. Click the link to approve directly in your browser. The link expires after 7 days.

**Q: Are internal notes visible to the contact?**
A: No. Notes are only visible to team members in your company. They are clearly marked with a yellow "internal note" label.

**Q: Can I @mention someone in a public reply?**
A: No. @mentions only work in note mode to keep internal discussions private. In a public reply, type the person's handle manually if needed.

**Q: How do I know if my teammate has seen a conversation?**
A: Look for the small avatar icons next to the conversation in the inbox list. Hover over them to see names and timestamps.

**Q: What happens when a post is rejected?**
A: It returns to Draft status and the author receives a notification with the reviewer's feedback. The author can edit the post and submit it again.
