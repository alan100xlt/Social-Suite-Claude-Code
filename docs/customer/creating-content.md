# Creating Content

Social Suite's compose wizard walks you through creating social media posts -- either from an article in your RSS feeds or from scratch. AI generates a content strategy and platform-optimized drafts that you can edit, approve, schedule, or publish.

---

## Starting a New Post

1. Go to **Content** in the left sidebar.
2. Make sure the **Compose** tab is selected.
3. Choose your starting point:
   - **From Article** -- pick an article from your RSS feeds as the source material.
   - **From Scratch** -- write your own post without a source article.

## Composing From an Article

### Step 1: Select an Article

After choosing **From Article**, you see a searchable list of recent articles pulled from your RSS feeds. Each article shows its title, publication date, and source feed.

Click an article to select it. If the article has an image, it is automatically attached to your post.

<!-- screenshot: The article selection list showing article titles, dates, and feed names -->

### Step 2: Choose Your Objective

Pick what you want this post to achieve:

- **Reach** -- maximize visibility and shares
- **Engagement** -- spark conversation and interaction
- **Clicks** -- drive traffic to your link

The AI uses your objective to shape the tone, call to action, and structure of the generated content.

### Step 3: Select Channels

Choose which connected social media accounts should receive this post. You can select multiple accounts across different platforms. The AI will generate a separate, optimized version for each platform.

If you have not connected any accounts yet, example/demo accounts are shown so you can preview the workflow. Go to **Connections** to add real accounts before publishing.

<!-- screenshot: The channel selection step showing connected platform accounts with checkboxes -->

### Step 4: Compose and Preview

This is where the AI does the heavy lifting. The process has three phases:

**Strategy Phase**
The AI generates a content strategy based on your article, objective, and selected platforms. Review the strategy and either:
- Click **Approve** to move forward with post generation.
- Use the **quick action buttons** ("Make it more casual", "Add more urgency", "Focus on data/stats", "Shorter and punchier") to refine the strategy.
- Type your own feedback in the chat box to steer the AI in a specific direction.

<!-- screenshot: The strategy phase showing the AI-generated strategy with Approve button and quick action chips -->

**Generation Phase**
After you approve the strategy, the AI generates platform-specific post text for each selected channel. A compliance check runs automatically to ensure content meets platform guidelines.

**Editing Phase**
You land on the editing view with a tab for each platform. Here you can:
- Edit the generated text for any platform directly in the text area.
- Switch between platforms using the tabs at the top.
- Upload or change the attached image.
- Click **Regenerate All** to have the AI create fresh versions.
- Click **Back to Strategy** to revise the strategy and regenerate.

<!-- screenshot: The editing phase showing per-platform tabs with editable text areas and a post preview -->

## Composing From Scratch

The flow is the same as above, but without the article selection step:

1. **Choose your objective** (Reach, Engagement, or Clicks).
2. **Select channels.**
3. **Compose and preview** -- the AI generates a strategy and drafts based on your objective alone. You can type your own content directly in the editing phase.

---

## Adding Images

You can attach an image to your post in two ways:

- **From an article** -- if the selected article has an image, it is automatically attached.
- **Upload your own** -- click the image upload area or the upload button in the compose step. Supported formats include JPG, PNG, and GIF.
- **Media Library** -- select from previously uploaded images.

To remove an attached image, click the **X** button on the image preview.

---

## Link as First Comment

For **Instagram** posts, the article link is added as a first comment instead of in the post body (since Instagram does not support clickable links in captions). This is enabled by default for Instagram and can be toggled for Facebook as well.

---

## Saving as a Draft

Your progress is **saved automatically** as you move through each step of the wizard. You can leave at any time and come back later.

To find your saved drafts:
1. Go to **Content** > **Drafts** tab.
2. Each draft shows the source type (article or scratch), current step, objective, and number of platforms.
3. Click **Continue** to pick up where you left off.

To delete a draft, click the trash icon on the draft card and confirm.

## Content Approval Workflow

Before publishing, you can send posts for approval:

1. In the compose step, click **Send for Approval**.
2. Add the email addresses of the people who should review the post. Team members from your company are suggested automatically, and you are included by default.
3. Click **Send**. Each reviewer receives an email with the post content and can approve or reject it.

Reviewers can also approve posts via an external approval link -- no login required.

### Workflow Statuses

- **Draft** -- still being edited
- **Awaiting Approval** -- submitted for review
- **Approved** -- reviewer approved; ready to publish
- **Rejected** -- reviewer rejected with an optional reason
- **Pulled** -- author withdrew the post from the approval queue

---

## Publishing and Scheduling

When your post is ready:

- **Publish Now** -- sends the post to all selected platforms immediately.
- **Schedule** -- pick a date and time using the calendar and time picker, then click schedule. The post will be published automatically at the chosen time.

A pre-publish validation check runs automatically. If there are errors (e.g., text exceeds a platform's character limit), publishing is blocked and you will see the specific issue. Warnings are shown but do not block publishing.

## Brand Voice

The AI adapts its output based on your company's **Brand Voice** settings (configured under **Settings** > **Brand Voice**). This includes tone, emoji style, hashtag strategy, and content length preferences. You do not need to configure anything for AI generation to work -- it will use sensible defaults -- but fine-tuning your brand voice produces more on-brand results.

---

## Tips

- **Start with an article if you have RSS feeds set up.** The AI produces much stronger content when it has source material to work from.
- **Use quick actions to iterate fast.** Instead of rewriting a strategy manually, click "Shorter and punchier" or "Focus on data/stats" to steer the AI.
- **Edit per platform.** A post that works on LinkedIn might need a different tone for Twitter. Use the per-platform tabs to customize each version.
- **Check the Drafts tab regularly.** Auto-saved drafts can pile up -- delete ones you no longer need.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I write a post entirely by hand without AI?**
Yes. Choose **From Scratch**, skip through to the compose step, and type your content directly in the text area for each platform.

**Q: How does the AI know my brand's tone?**
It uses your Brand Voice settings under **Settings** > **Brand Voice**. If you have not configured a brand voice, the AI uses a neutral, professional tone.

**Q: What happens if I close the browser mid-compose?**
Your draft is auto-saved. Reopen **Content** > **Drafts** and click **Continue** on the saved draft.

**Q: Can I publish to some platforms now and schedule others for later?**
Not in a single compose session. To publish at different times, create separate posts for each timing.

**Q: What is the compliance check?**
After the AI generates post text, an automated compliance check reviews the content against platform guidelines and adjusts if needed. This happens automatically -- you do not need to do anything.
