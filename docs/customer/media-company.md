# Media Companies

A media company in Social Suite is a parent organization that manages multiple publications or outlets. If you run a media group with several brands -- each with its own social accounts -- the media company dashboard gives you a bird's-eye view of the entire portfolio.

---

## What is a Media Company?

Think of a media company as an umbrella over your individual companies. For example:

- **Triangle Media Group** (the media company) might manage:
  - Holly Springs Update
  - Wake County News
  - Triangle Business Daily

Each of those outlets is a regular Social Suite company with its own social accounts, content, and analytics. The media company layer lets you see aggregate stats and manage the portfolio from one place.

---

## The Media Company Dashboard

To access a media company dashboard, go to the link provided by your admin or navigate to it from the **Company** tab in Settings (if your company is associated with a media company, you will see a **View** button).

<!-- screenshot: The media company dashboard header showing the media company name, Settings button, and Add Company button -->

### Portfolio Stats

At the top of the dashboard, four summary cards show aggregate metrics across all companies in the portfolio:

- **Portfolio Companies** -- the total number of active publications
- **Total Posts** -- posts published in the last 30 days across all outlets
- **Total Followers** -- combined follower count across all platforms and outlets
- **Total Engagement** -- total likes, comments, and shares

<!-- screenshot: The four portfolio stat cards showing company count, total posts, total followers, and total engagement -->

### Dashboard Views

Below the stats, three tabs let you explore different aspects of the portfolio:

#### Overview

Shows **Top Performing Companies** ranked by total followers. Each entry displays the company name, post count, engagement numbers, and follower total.

#### Companies

Lists all companies in the portfolio with:

- **Relationship type** -- a badge showing whether the company is Owned, Managed, or Partnered
- **Post count, follower count, and engagement** for each company
- A **View** button to jump to that company's dashboard

#### Members

Team member management for the media company. *This feature is coming soon.*

## Adding Companies to the Portfolio

Click the **Add Company** button in the top-right corner of the dashboard. You have two options:

### Create a New Company

1. Select the **Create New** tab.
2. Enter a **Name** for the new company.
3. The **URL Slug** is auto-generated from the name. You can customize it.
4. Optionally add a **Website** and **Description**.
5. Click **Create & Add**. The new company is created and immediately added to the portfolio.

### Add an Existing Company

1. Select the **Add Existing** tab.
2. Search for the company by name in the search field.
3. Select it from the list.
4. Choose a **Relationship Type**:
   - **Owned** -- the media company fully owns this outlet
   - **Managed** -- the media company manages this outlet's social presence
   - **Partnered** -- a partnership arrangement
5. Click **Add to Portfolio**.

## Enterprise Workspace

The **Workspace** is an advanced view for managing larger portfolios. It provides:

- **Search and filter** -- find companies by name, filter by your role (Admin, Member, Viewer), and sort by name, followers, posts, or engagement.
- **Company cards** -- each company shows follower count, post count, engagement rate, and last active time. Click a card to switch to that company.
- **Quick actions** -- buttons for Bulk Content (create posts for multiple outlets at once) and Team management.

Additional tabs in the workspace (Analytics, Content, and Team) provide portfolio-wide views. *Some of these features are still being expanded.*

## Tips

- **Check the Overview tab regularly.** It gives you a quick read on which outlets are performing best and might need more attention.

- **Use relationship types meaningfully.** If you manage outlets you do not own, mark them as "Managed" or "Partnered" to keep the portfolio organized.

- **Start by adding existing companies.** If your outlets already have Social Suite accounts, add them to the media company rather than creating new ones. This preserves their existing content and analytics history.

## FAQ

**Q: Who can access the media company dashboard?**
A: You need to be a member of the media company. Access is controlled by your media company administrator. If you see an "Access Denied" message, contact your admin.

**Q: Does adding a company to a media company change anything for that company?**
A: No. The individual company keeps all its existing settings, content, and connections. The media company layer is an overlay for portfolio-level visibility.

**Q: Can a company belong to multiple media companies?**
A: Typically, a company belongs to one media company. Contact support if you have a special case.

**Q: What is the difference between the Dashboard and the Workspace?**
A: The Dashboard is a read-only overview of portfolio performance. The Workspace provides tools for searching, filtering, and managing companies, with quick actions for bulk content and team management.
