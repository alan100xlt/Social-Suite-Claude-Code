## General Email Rules

- **Subject lines**: 6-10 words, specific benefit or curiosity gap. Never ALL CAPS or exclamation marks.
- **Preview text**: First 40-90 chars visible in inbox. Don't waste it on "View in browser."
- **From name**: Use a person's name ("Alan from Longtale") not just "Longtale.ai"
- **One CTA per email**: Multiple CTAs dilute click-through. Pick the single most important action.
- **Mobile-first**: 60%+ of emails read on mobile. Short paragraphs, large buttons, no tiny links.
- **Send time**: Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11am recipient's local time for B2B.
- **Unsubscribe**: Always visible. Never hide it. Legal requirement (CAN-SPAM, GDPR).

## Onboarding Drip Sequence

Goal: Guide new users from signup to "aha moment" (seeing AI-generated posts from their RSS feed).

### Email 1 — Welcome (immediate, on signup)
**Subject**: "Your Longtale.ai account is ready"
**Content**:
- Welcome + confirmation
- One clear next step: "Connect your first RSS feed"
- Link directly to the RSS connection page (deep link, not homepage)
- Set expectation: "Over the next few days, I'll show you how to get the most out of Longtale"
- Sign off from a real person (founder or head of customer success)

### Email 2 — First value action (Day 1, if user hasn't connected RSS)
**Subject**: "Your first AI-generated posts are one click away"
**Content**:
- Remind them of the core value prop
- Step-by-step: connect RSS -> AI generates posts -> review and schedule
- Include a screenshot/GIF of what it looks like
- CTA: "Connect your RSS feed now"

### Email 3 — Social proof (Day 3)
**Subject**: "How [Customer Name] manages 12 brands with a 2-person team"
**Content**:
- Brief case study or testimonial
- Focus on a specific, impressive result
- Tie it to a feature they haven't tried yet
- CTA: "See what's possible" -> link to relevant feature

### Email 4 — Feature discovery (Day 5)
**Subject**: "Did you know you can [underused feature]?"
**Content**:
- Highlight one feature based on what they HAVEN'T used yet
- Cross-platform scheduling, approval workflows, or analytics
- Short explanation + screenshot
- CTA: "Try it now" -> deep link to that feature

### Email 5 — Check-in (Day 7)
**Subject**: "How's it going with Longtale?"
**Content**:
- Personal, conversational tone
- Ask if they have questions or need help
- Offer a 15-minute call or live chat
- Include link to help docs and community
- CTA: "Reply to this email — I read every one"

### Email 6 — Recap + upgrade nudge (Day 10, if on free trial)
**Subject**: "Your trial recap: [X posts generated, Y hours saved]"
**Content**:
- Personalized stats from their trial usage
- Compare to industry average: "You're publishing 3x more than the average media company"
- Gentle upgrade CTA: "Keep the momentum — upgrade before your trial ends"
- Address pricing objection: "Plans start at $X/month for unlimited brands"

## Trial-to-Paid Conversion

### 3-email sequence starting 5 days before trial end

**Email T-5: Value recap**
Subject: "You've generated [N] posts this week — here's what happens next"
- Show personalized usage metrics
- Highlight what they'd lose: "Your scheduled posts, analytics history, and brand settings"
- CTA: "Upgrade now to keep everything"

**Email T-2: Urgency + objection handling**
Subject: "2 days left — quick question"
- Ask: "Is there anything holding you back?"
- List and address top 3 objections:
  1. Price -> "Plans start at $X, and you save [calculated hours/month]"
  2. Not sure yet -> "Extend your trial for 7 days — just reply to this email"
  3. Missing feature -> "Tell us what you need — we ship weekly"
- CTA: "Upgrade now" or "Reply with questions"

**Email T-0: Last day**
Subject: "Your trial ends today"
- Clear, honest: what happens when trial expires
- Reassure: "Your data is safe for 30 days. Upgrade anytime to pick up where you left off"
- Final CTA: "Keep your account active"
- No guilt trips or fake urgency

## Re-Engagement Sequence

Trigger: User hasn't logged in for 14+ days.

### Email 1 (Day 14 inactive)
**Subject**: "We miss you (and so does your content calendar)"
- Light, friendly tone
- Show what they're missing: "You have [N] unscheduled posts waiting"
- One-click return: deep link to their dashboard
- CTA: "See what's waiting for you"

### Email 2 (Day 21 inactive)
**Subject**: "What could we do better?"
- Genuine feedback request
- Short 1-question survey: "What's the #1 reason you stopped using Longtale?"
  - Too complex
  - Missing feature I need
  - Found another tool
  - Don't need it right now
  - Other
- CTA: "Take 10-second survey"

### Email 3 (Day 30 inactive)
**Subject**: "New since you've been away: [top feature]"
- Highlight the best new feature or improvement since they left
- Brief demo or screenshot
- Offer: "Come back and try it — your account is exactly how you left it"
- CTA: "Log back in"

### Email 4 (Day 45 inactive — final)
**Subject**: "Should we close your account?"
- Honest, no-pressure
- "We'll keep your data for 90 days. After that, we'll clean things up."
- Option to reactivate or download their data
- This email typically gets the highest re-engagement rate due to loss aversion

## Feature Announcement

Template for announcing new features to existing users:

**Subject**: "[New] [Feature name] — [one-line benefit]"
Example: "[New] AI Brand Voice — your posts now sound like YOUR brand"

**Structure**:
1. **What's new** (1-2 sentences, plain language)
2. **Why it matters to you** (1 sentence, tied to their use case)
3. **How it works** (3 bullet points or a GIF)
4. **Try it now** (deep link CTA)

**Rules**:
- Don't announce everything at once — one feature per email
- Send only to users who would benefit (segment by usage)
- Include a "not interested? tell us what you'd rather see" feedback link
- Time feature announcements Tuesday-Wednesday for highest open rates

## Newsletter / Content Marketing Emails

For Longtale.ai's content marketing emails:

**Frequency**: Bi-weekly or monthly. Never more than weekly.

**Structure**:
1. **One key insight** (2-3 paragraphs) — social media trend, data finding, or strategy
2. **Quick tips** (3 bullet points) — actionable, specific
3. **Customer spotlight** (1 paragraph) — brief case study or quote
4. **Product update** (1 line) — only if relevant to the insight
5. **CTA** — read full blog post, try a feature, or share feedback

**Subject line formulas**:
- "We analyzed [N] posts: here's what works for [industry]"
- "[Number] [topic] tips from media companies that get it right"
- "The [topic] mistake 90% of publishers make"
- "How [Customer] [achieved specific result]"
