## Hero Section Deep Dive

### Header Writing — Value Prop Exercise

For any product, answer these three questions in order:

1. **What bad alternative do people resort to without your product?**
   - Longtale.ai example: "Logging into 5 different social platforms for each of 10 brands, copy-pasting content, manually adapting format"

2. **How is your product better than that alternative?**
   - "One dashboard, AI adapts content per platform, schedule once for all brands"

3. **Turn step 2 into an action statement** = your value prop
   - "Manage all your brands' social media from one AI-powered dashboard"

### Header Anti-Patterns (never do these)

| Bad Pattern | Why It Fails | Fix |
|------------|-------------|-----|
| "Supercharge your workflow!" | Vague slogan, no product clarity | Describe what you actually sell |
| "The #1 Platform for Growth" | Self-congratulatory, unverifiable | Talk in terms of visitor benefit |
| "Welcome to Longtale" | Says nothing about value | Lead with the value prop |
| "Revolutionize Your Social Media" | Corporate speak, no specifics | Add specific outcome or metric |
| "AI-Powered Excellence" | Buzzword soup | Describe the specific AI capability |

### Hook Types with Longtale.ai Examples

**Bold claim hook:**
- "Turn 1 RSS article into 10 posts across 5 platforms in under 60 seconds"
- "Media companies using Longtale.ai publish 8x more social content with the same team size"
- "The only social media tool built specifically for multi-brand media companies"

**Objection-handling hook:**
Common objections for Longtale.ai:
- "AI content won't match our brand voice" -> "AI trained on YOUR brand voice from your existing content"
- "We need approval workflows for compliance" -> "Built-in approval chains — editors, legal, and management sign off before anything goes live"
- "We've tried tools before, onboarding takes forever" -> "Connect your RSS feed and social accounts — AI generates your first posts in 5 minutes"
- "It's too expensive for multiple brands" -> "One price for unlimited brands. No per-seat surprises"

### Subheader Formulas

**Formula 1 — What + How:**
> [What the product is] + [How the bold claim is possible]
> "Longtale.ai connects to your RSS feeds and uses AI to generate platform-optimized posts — then schedules them across X, LinkedIn, Instagram, and Facebook from a single team dashboard."

**Formula 2 — Pain + Solution:**
> [Pain you eliminate] + [How you eliminate it]
> "Stop copy-pasting the same story across 5 platforms. Longtale.ai auto-generates each post in each platform's native format."

**Formula 3 — Persona + Outcome:**
> [For whom] + [specific outcome]
> "Built for media companies managing 5+ brands. Publish 10x more social content without hiring more people."

### Choose-Your-Own-Adventure Pattern

When your product serves multiple distinct personas, let visitors self-select at the top:

```
[For Media Companies]     [For Individual Publishers]     [For Agencies]
```

Each button scrolls to or routes to a section tailored to that persona's concerns. This prevents the "this isn't for me" bounce.

## Feature Section Patterns

Each feature section follows this structure:
1. **Header** — Short value prop (not vague, not a slogan)
2. **Paragraph** — Explain the feature + handle one objection
3. **Image/GIF** — Show the product in action

### Feature Writing Rules

- Feature headers should be scannable — a visitor who reads ONLY the feature headers should understand all your key value props
- Each feature paragraph should tie back to the hero's dominant value prop
- Address one objection per feature — the most likely objection that arises from hearing that value prop
- If a feature needs extensive explanation, add a "Learn more" expand button rather than bloating the section

## CTA Patterns

### CTA Copy Rules

The CTA should feel like the natural next step to the hero's promise:
- Hero says "Publish everywhere" -> CTA says "Start publishing"
- Hero says "Save 15 hours/week" -> CTA says "See how much time you'll save"

### CTA Examples for Longtale.ai

| Location | Primary CTA | Secondary CTA |
|----------|------------|---------------|
| Hero | "Start free trial" | "See a demo" |
| After features | "Try it free — no credit card" | "Talk to sales" |
| After testimonials | "Join 500+ media companies" | "See pricing" |
| Footer | "Get started free" | "Contact us" |

### CTA Anti-Patterns

- "Submit" — meaningless, creates anxiety
- "Request a demo" without first explaining the product — visitors don't know what they're requesting
- "Learn more" as a primary CTA — too passive, low commitment
- Multiple equal-weight CTAs in the hero — pick ONE primary action

## Image Guidelines

- **Show the product in action** — screenshots, GIFs of workflows, annotated UI
- **Blur irrelevant details** — focus attention on the feature being highlighted
- **Use real (or realistic) data** — the demo company data is perfect for screenshots
- **Avoid abstract stock imagery** — no handshakes, no lightbulbs, no puzzle pieces
- **Alternate image placement** — left/right/left/right as you scroll down features
- **Mobile screenshots** — include mobile views for responsive credibility

## Page Review Criteria

After building a landing page, get feedback on six criteria:

1. **Conversion** — "Would you sign up right now? If not, what's missing?"
2. **Interest** — "Rate how well the page sustained your interest (1-10)"
3. **Clarity** — "What's unclear? What questions do you still have?"
4. **Expansion** — "What did you want MORE detail on?"
5. **Brevity** — "If you had to cut half the page, what would you remove?"
6. **Disbelief** — "What triggered your 'that sounds like BS' reflex?"

Get feedback from TWO groups:
- **People NOT in your market** — tests comprehensibility and general appeal
- **People IN your market** — tests competitive differentiation and messaging accuracy

## Longtale.ai Landing Page Variants

The codebase has multiple landing page variants at `/`, `/v2`, `/v3`, `/v4`. When building a new variant:

1. Study existing variants in `src/pages/` (LandingPage.tsx and variants)
2. Reuse components from `src/components/landing/`
3. Add the route in `src/App.tsx` under public routes
4. Track variant performance with PostHog: `posthog.capture('landing_variant_view', { variant: 'v5' })`
5. Run A/B tests by randomly routing visitors to variants and comparing signup rates
