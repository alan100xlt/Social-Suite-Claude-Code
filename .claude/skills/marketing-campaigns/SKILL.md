## Landing Page Framework

The core formula: `Purchase Rate = Desire - (Labor + Confusion)`

- **Increase desire** — Show specific value, create intrigue
- **Decrease labor** — Be concise, every word earns its place
- **Decrease confusion** — Make it obvious what you sell and what action to take

### Page Structure (use this order)

1. **Navbar** — Logo + navigation links
2. **Hero** — Header + subheader + captivating imagery
3. **Social proof** — Press logos, customer logos, or user count
4. **CTA** — Signup button with concise incentive
5. **Features & objections** — 3-6 value props with objection handling
6. **Repeat CTA**
7. **Footer**

### Hero Writing Process

**Step 1 — Find your value prop:**
1. What bad alternative do people resort to without your product?
2. How is your product better than that alternative?
3. Turn that into an action statement = your value prop

**Step 2 — Write a descriptive header:**
Litmus test: If a visitor reads ONLY the header, do they know exactly what you sell?

Bad: "Supercharge your social media!" (vague slogan)
Good: "AI-powered social media for media companies. Generate and schedule posts across all your brands from one dashboard."

**Step 3 — Add a hook** (pick one):
- **Bold claim**: Specific, triggers "wow, I didn't know that was possible"
  - "Turn one RSS article into 10 platform-optimized posts in 30 seconds"
- **Objection handling**: Address the #1 buying objection
  - "No design skills needed — AI generates platform-native visuals automatically"

**Step 4 — Write the subheader** (1-2 sentences):
- Explain how the product works
- Make the bold claim believable
- Example: "Longtale.ai connects to your RSS feeds, uses AI to generate platform-optimized posts, and schedules them across X, LinkedIn, Instagram, and Facebook — all managed from a single dashboard your whole team can use."

For the full landing page framework including feature sections, CTA patterns, images, and page review criteria, see [references/landing-pages.md](references/landing-pages.md).

## Social Proof Patterns

Ordered by persuasion strength (strongest first):

1. **Specific metrics** — "Used by 500+ media companies publishing 50,000 posts/month"
2. **Named customer logos** — Recognizable media brand logos in a horizontal strip
3. **Testimonial quotes** — Real quotes with name, title, photo, company logo
4. **Case studies** — "DiarioJudio increased social engagement 340% in 3 months"
5. **Press logos** — "As seen in TechCrunch, MediaPost, Nieman Lab"
6. **User count** — "Join 10,000+ social media managers" (only if number is impressive)
7. **Awards/badges** — G2, Capterra, Product Hunt badges

### Testimonial Formula

> "[Specific result achieved] since switching to Longtale.ai. [What they tried before that failed]. [Specific feature they love most]."
> — Name, Title, Company (with headshot)

**Rules:**
- Always include specific numbers ("saved 15 hours/week" not "saved time")
- Always include the person's photo — faceless testimonials reduce trust
- Place testimonials near the feature they validate
- Use 3 testimonials minimum, 6 maximum on a single page

### Case Study Structure

1. **Challenge** — What problem the customer faced (1 paragraph)
2. **Solution** — How they used Longtale.ai (1 paragraph + screenshot)
3. **Results** — Specific metrics with before/after (bullet list)
4. **Quote** — Pull quote from the customer

## Conversion Optimization

### A/B Testing Priority (test these first)

1. **Hero headline** — Highest impact element. Test value prop angle, not word tweaks
2. **CTA text and placement** — "Start free trial" vs "See it in action" vs "Get started free"
3. **Social proof type** — Logos vs testimonials vs metrics
4. **Pricing page structure** — Feature comparison vs use-case tiers
5. **Form length** — Email-only vs email+company vs full form

### Funnel Analysis

Track these conversion points:

```
Landing page visit
  -> CTA click (target: 5-15% CTR)
    -> Signup form start (target: 60-80% of clickers)
      -> Signup complete (target: 70-90% of starters)
        -> Onboarding complete (target: 40-60%)
          -> First value action (target: 30-50%)
            -> Paid conversion (target: 3-8% of signups)
```

**First value action** for Longtale.ai = user connects a social account AND publishes or schedules their first post.

### Friction Reduction Checklist

- [ ] Can users sign up with just email? (no company name, phone, etc. required upfront)
- [ ] Is there a "try without signup" option? (demo mode using demo company data)
- [ ] Does onboarding show progress? (step X of Y)
- [ ] Can users reach first value action in under 5 minutes?
- [ ] Are error messages specific and actionable?
- [ ] Is the pricing page clear with no hidden fees?
- [ ] Can users cancel without contacting support?

## Product-Led Growth

### PLA Types Applied to Longtale.ai

1. **User invitations** — When a media company admin invites editors to collaborate, those editors must create accounts. Each company hierarchy multiplies this effect.

2. **Billboarding** — Add "Powered by Longtale.ai" to:
   - Shared analytics reports
   - Approval request emails sent to external stakeholders
   - Public-facing content calendars
   - Social post preview links

3. **User-generated content** — Analytics dashboards and reports that users share internally become product demos. Make sharing frictionless.

4. **Word of mouth** — Trigger by:
   - Reducing pain (scheduling across 5 platforms in one click vs logging into each)
   - Delighting (AI generates posts that actually sound good)
   - Saving measurable time (show "15 hours saved this month" in dashboard)

### Retention via State Building

For Longtale.ai, the strongest retention levers are:

- **Non-transferrable data** — Historical analytics, post performance data, audience insights that accumulate over months
- **Embedded infrastructure** — Connected social accounts, RSS feeds, brand voice settings, approval workflows — all painful to recreate elsewhere
- **Team social graph** — Team members, roles, permissions, approval chains

### Activation Metrics

Define and track these for Longtale.ai:

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Time to first post | Minutes from signup to first scheduled/published post | < 10 min |
| Day-1 retention | % of signups who return within 24 hours | > 40% |
| Week-1 activation | % who connect account + schedule 3+ posts in first 7 days | > 25% |
| Aha moment | User sees AI-generated post from their RSS feed for first time | Track time-to-aha |

**Primary keywords** (high intent):
- "social media management for publishers"
- "media company social media tool"

**Long-tail keywords** (lower competition):

After publishing a blog post:
1. Create 3-5 social posts from the article (use the content-generation skill)
2. Send to email list with key takeaway as hook
3. Cross-post to LinkedIn as a condensed article
4. Share in relevant communities (media industry Slack groups, journalism subreddits)
5. Pitch as guest content to media industry newsletters

## Building Marketing Pages in This Codebase

When implementing marketing pages in Social Suite:

1. **Route**: Add to `src/App.tsx` under public routes (not `/app/*`)
2. **Components**: Create in `src/components/landing/` — NOT in `src/components/ui/`
3. **Styling**: Use Tailwind CSS. Reference existing landing pages (`/v2`, `/v3`, `/v4`)
4. **Responsive**: Mobile-first. Test at 375px, 768px, 1024px, 1440px
5. **Performance**: Lazy-load below-fold images. Keep hero section under 200KB
6. **Analytics**: Track page views and CTA clicks with PostHog
7. **Demo data**: Use the demo company (`DEMO_COMPANY_ID`) for any interactive previews

### PostHog Event Naming for Marketing

```typescript
posthog.capture('marketing_cta_clicked', {
  page: 'landing-v5',
  cta_location: 'hero' | 'features' | 'pricing' | 'footer',
  cta_text: 'Start free trial'
})

posthog.capture('marketing_page_scroll', {
  page: 'landing-v5',
  scroll_depth: '25%' | '50%' | '75%' | '100%'
})
```
